# Disclaimer, Scope & Methodology

## Nature of this archive

This repository is a **research archive** documenting a matter of public interest. It is **not** legal
advice, not journalism with independent fact-finding, and not an endorsement of any party's position.
It aggregates and organizes **already-public** reporting and records.

## Allegation vs. fact

This dispute is **actively contested** and involves **ongoing civil litigation and a pending criminal
matter**. Throughout these documents:

- **CONFIRMED** marks facts documented by court records, official agency statements, or multiple
  independent reputable outlets (e.g., that a lawsuit was filed; that arrests occurred).
- **ALLEGATION** marks contentions advanced by one side and **not** adjudicated (e.g., that any party
  "stole" the collection; that police acted with bias; that the campaign was "extortion").

As of the compilation date (2026-06-03), **no court has found any party civilly liable or criminally
guilty** in this matter. Criminal charges are accusations; a defendant is presumed innocent unless and
until proven guilty. Civil complaint allegations are unproven contentions.

## What is deliberately excluded

To keep this archive ethical, lawful, and credible, it **excludes**:

- **Personal identifying information (PII)** about any individual — private citizens *and* named
  police officers alike: no home addresses, personal phone numbers, personal email addresses, family
  member details, daily-movement information, or anything that could facilitate harassment, stalking,
  or "doxxing."
- **Re-hosted copyrighted media.** News photographs and videos are **linked to their original
  sources**, not copied into this repository.
- **Unverified rumor presented as fact.** Single-source or clearly partisan claims are flagged.

Note that **doxxing, harassment, impersonation, and forged documents are themselves causes of action /
allegations within the BAM lawsuit.** An archive that engaged in PII-aggregation would risk
reproducing the very conduct in dispute and would undermine its own value as a neutral record.

## Methodology & limitations

- Built from **WebSearch result summaries** of the cited reputable outlets plus the parties' public
  statements. The compilation environment had **no outbound page-fetch access** (HTTP 403,
  `host_not_allowed`), so full article text could not be independently opened during compilation.
- Some **dates are approximate** ("approx.") and some **author bylines are unconfirmed**; these are
  flagged inline. Re-verify against the live sources before relying on any specific detail.
- **Name spellings vary across outlets** (e.g., "Bryan Mansell" vs. "Brian Mancel"; one outlet
  rendered Schneider's surname incorrectly). The most consistently reported forms are used here.
- The **case number** is reported as **260402353** by news outlets; one community site rendered it
  "260400253" (likely a transcription error). Verify against the primary docket.

## Corrections

If you can document an error against a primary source, correct it and cite the source. Prefer primary
records (court filings, full agency statements, full original videos) over aggregated summaries.
